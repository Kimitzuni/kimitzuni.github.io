All Phones Are Annoying

January, 17th 2024

All phones are designed to be annoying in very oddly specific ways. Sure you can argue and contest this by showing the benefits they have had on humans as a whole, but additionally we can also point to the disadvantages that they have had, such as people no longer want to be a part of the world that is right outside their windows, and would much rather spend hours and hours on TikTok, YouTube, Snapchat and Instagram. Which is a shame, because nature is great when you actually are focused on it. So, you may be thinking that this is a weird opinion to have right, especially from an 18 year old. Well, no not really, not when you actually think about it.

On my phones, I don't like the default Samsung firmware, because OneUI is a horrible, bloated mess and I also don't trust OneUI and Samsung's firmware. So I choose to install LineageOS on my devices, and for devices such as the Samsung Galaxy S7 (herolte), this is fine - as LineageOS builds for herolte exist. However, let's switch the tables to a low end Samsung device. Where a LineageOS build doesn't exist. For example - Samsung Galaxy A12 (a12s). Luckily on devices without a build, we can use a Generic System Image (GSI). But wait! A GSI is just the equivalent of the system.img file. Samsung's modern firmwares use super.img, which combines several of the images into one, so we can't just flash a system.img file anymore, we instead have to unpack the super.img and repack it with our new system.img in there. Easy?

But wait! There's more! Samsung Knox. Samsung Knox is one of the most infurating things I have ever had to deal with. Knox claims to make your device safer, however — I disagree. It is only there to make your life more difficult if you ever decide to actually do something with your phone. It will fight you every. single. step of the way. Knox has a security check to see if your phone has been modified, and if it has, it does a few things. 1 - it voids your warranty so good luck with that. 2 - you can no longer use certain features of your phone, such as contactless payment. Samsung, you need to understand this: if I am unlocking the bootloader — I know what I'm doing. Stop trying to fight your users. It makes you look like Apple.

Ironically, the easiest phones to de-Google and install a custom ROM onto are the Google Pixel phones.

People are probably going to suggest something like "well get a Google Pixel", "Buy an iPhone!", "buy a Microsoft phone, I have lots of apps I swear". And, I don't want to. Because it just takes a cursory glance at the market to understand that phone design and features have peaked a long time ago.

  • Google — They release a new Pixel phone like every 6 months or something, so the previous model never gets a chance to fall in price, and they're all in on AI, so the phones are expensive;

  • Samsung — Their phones will fight you every step of the way if they can. Low and mid range Samsung Galaxy devices are awful, and the good phones are the Galaxy S and Z phones which are quite often in the range of Â£1000+;

  • Huawei — Essentially just copying the phone designs of Samsung;
  • Nokia — Low budget Android devices, as Windows Mobile was a massive flop;
  • Research In Motion - BlackBerry — Rest In Peace;
  • Apple — No.

The mobile phone market also finds itself in the unique situation of everyone wanting to innovate, but everyone is copying each other so no actual innovation is getting done. The last big things in phone design were the Galaxy Note's pen, biometric unlocking, the removal of physical home buttons, the removal of the headphone jack and making the front-facing camera part of the screen.

Phones are especially annoying nowadays if you want to repair it yourself. Even if you just need a new battery, replacing it yourself is near impossible. Let's go back just 12 years to the Galaxy S3. That had a removable back cover and removable battery. If your battery started to degrade and not hold as much charge, you simply bought a new one online (or in person, we hadn't yet become that degenerate), and you simply took the old one out and put the new one in. A very simple process. But now, you either have to be confident enough to use a heat gun on the back of a phone near the battery, or take it to someone who is - and that isn't cheap either.

I believe that this stems from the iPhone, as since the beginning of the iPhone in 2007, Apple never wanted you going inside of it. In at least iPhone 4 - SE, you could take the screen out yourself (I don't know if it was like this on any other models), but fundamentally you still had to know what type of screwdriver you needed, as it wasn't a standard + or - screw. That would be way too easy.

Summary

Every phone is basically the same. Blame everyone.
